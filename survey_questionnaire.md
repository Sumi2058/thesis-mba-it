## Section 4: Product Quality Mismatch

> **Source:** Vaidya (2019) found product quality to be the single most common problem in Nepalese online shopping, including delivery of wrong or substandard products. Devkota et al. Tanjung, Sumarwan, & Krisnatuti (2025) confirmed that product mismatches, damaged goods, and misleading descriptions drive e-commerce consumer dissatisfaction. Izumi, Setiawan, & Ghaffar (n.d.) found that poor quality and wrong items delivered were the most frequent return reasons among online shoppers.

## Section 5: Risk Perception

> **Source:** Pandey (2025) empirically established that financial and product risk significantly influence online buying behaviour in Nepal. Pavlou (2003) proposed that perceived risk — encompassing monetary loss, privacy exposure, and uncertainty — is a core determinant of e-commerce acceptance. Kim et al. (2008) demonstrated that perceived risk has a strong negative impact on consumer purchase decisions in electronic commerce. Hipólito et al. (2025) confirmed a direct relationship between risk perception and online shopping satisfaction. Bianchi & Andrews (2012) found that perceived risk had an inverse relationship with consumer attitude and purchase intention.
